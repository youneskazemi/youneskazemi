# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary visitors are **Iran-based freelance clients** — business owners and operators who find Younes via Karlancer, Telegram, or referrals. They open the site on phone or laptop between work tasks, often in Persian first, and need to decide quickly whether he can build a company site, WooCommerce shop, or custom Next.js + Django product.

Job to be done: evaluate real past work, understand how engagement works (phased delivery), then start a conversation without friction.

## Product Purpose

This is a personal freelance portfolio for **Younes Kazemi (سیدیونس کاظمی)**. It exists to showcase shipped client work and convert serious visitors into Telegram or email contact for a phase-by-phase estimate.

Success looks like: a qualified visitor contacts with a clear site type (company / store / custom), after seeing live projects and trusting the delivery process.

## Positioning

Real products for real businesses — shops, platforms, and custom web — delivered in clear freelance phases, not mockups or template demos.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: primary is **contact** (Telegram preferred, email for briefs); secondary is **browse selected work** and the full `/projects` catalog when they are not ready to message.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: real shipped sites and shops, with clear phases and a simple way to start.
- Belief ladder: (1) this is a serious developer with live work, not a template gallery; (2) the work matches the kind of site I need; (3) engagement is phased and understandable; (4) contacting is low-friction (Telegram / email).
- Proof on hand: live project URLs, browser-framed screenshot covers under `public/projects/covers/`, FA/EN case-study copy in `content/projects.ts`. No formal testimonials or partner-logo pack yet.

## Brand Personality

**Clear · capable · direct.** Practical freelaner energy: show the work, state the paths (WordPress/Woo vs custom), make the next step obvious. Confidence without agency fluff. Bilingual voice stays plain and professional in both فارسی and English — no hype metrics, no empty superlatives.

Emotional goal: trust and competence under a calm dark surface; urgency only where contact is invited, not everywhere.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI SaaS dark templates — purple glows, hero-metric rows, identical icon cards, startup landing clichés that could belong to any tool.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't claim** — live links and real screenshots carry proof; copy stays short and specific.
2. **Practice what you ship** — the portfolio itself should feel as careful as client work (contrast, touch targets, motion restraint).
3. **Path over pitch** — two clear service paths and a phased process beat feature laundry lists.
4. **FA-first hospitality** — Persian is the default experience; English is a peer, not an afterthought.
5. **One clear next step** — every major surface points either to work or to contact, never into dead ends.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Solid practical accessibility: visible focus rings, body contrast on dark surfaces, ≥44px touch targets on primary controls, and full respect for `prefers-reduced-motion`. No formal WCAG certification target unless a future client engagement requires it. Support bilingual RTL/LTR without trapping keyboard or screen-reader users in broken order.
